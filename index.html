Purpose: This portal provides a structured pathway for QEERI centers to submit VirtuaLab development requests for review, prioritization, and implementation.

Governance Principles

The process is designed to support fair intake, clear review, and center-driven prioritization.

Center priority

Up to two priority VirtuaLabs

Each QEERI center may nominate up to two high-value VirtuaLabs representing its most important projects, tools, or technologies.

Readiness review

Defined scope before development

Requests are reviewed based on project maturity, available technical material, expected outputs, and required development effort.

Transparent queue

Prioritized implementation

Requests are generally processed in submission order, with consideration for strategic value, readiness, and available resources.

Review Criteria

Complete submissions should clearly explain the problem, proposed tool, available technical material, required features, and expected value.

Technical Readiness

Availability of equations, models, datasets, code, Excel tools, reports, publications, figures, or prototype materials.

Expected Value

Potential contribution to research visibility, education, grant support, stakeholder engagement, commercialization, or technology demonstration.

Development Clarity

Clear definition of user inputs, outputs, dashboard requirements, scenario comparisons, calculations, and validation needs.

Confidentiality and IP

Identification of patent-related, confidential, restricted, or public-facing content before development and deployment.

Request Workflow

Each request moves through submission, review, development, validation, and deployment.

1

Request submission

The center director or assigned focal person submits the proposed VirtuaLab request.

2

Initial screening

The request is checked for completeness, scope clarity, technical readiness, and supporting material.

3

Feasibility review

The coordination team reviews technical feasibility, confidentiality requirements, expected impact, and development effort.

4

Prototype development

Approved requests are developed into interactive prototypes for review by the submitting center.

5

Validation and deployment

The center validates the prototype before final deployment as part of the QEERI VirtuaLab ecosystem.

Frequently Asked Questions

Key points for center directors and focal persons.

Who should submit a request?

Requests should be submitted by the center director or an assigned focal person who can provide technical details and coordinate validation.

How many VirtuaLabs can each center nominate?

Each center may initially nominate up to two priority VirtuaLabs to keep the intake process focused and manageable.

Will every request be developed?

Requests will be reviewed for completeness, technical readiness, feasibility, confidentiality, and expected value before development begins.

Can confidential or patent-related tools be submitted?

Yes. Any confidential, restricted, or patent-related content should be clearly identified in the request form for appropriate review before deployment.

What happens after submission?

The request will be reviewed by the QEERI VirtuaLab coordination team. The submitting focal person may be contacted for clarification, validation material, or prototype review.
